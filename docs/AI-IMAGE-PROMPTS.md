# RallyReady — AI Image Prompts for Every Diagram

A ready-to-use prompt book for generating **clean, friendly, production-ready
illustrations** to replace (or sit beside) the app's schematic SVG diagrams.
Hand these prompts to an image model (Midjourney, DALL·E 3, Ideogram, Firefly,
Imagen, Flux, etc.) to get clearer, more inviting pictures that a youth coach
can read at a glance on a phone.

Every diagram in RallyReady is built from a **small set of repeating
templates**, so this book is organized by template, not by drill. Each template
gets one master prompt plus notes for the few things that change between drills
(team size, number of servers, target zones, captions). Generate the template
once, then re-run with the small tweak when you need a specific drill's variant.

The three diagram families in the app:

| Family | Where it appears | Viewpoint | Section |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Drill court diagrams** | Today / Drills "how it's organized" cards | Top-down court | [Part 1](#part-1--drill-court-diagrams-top-down) |
| **Tips motion diagrams** | Tips screen ("show, don't tell") | Side-on action | [Part 2](#part-2--tips-motion-diagrams-side-view) |
| **Position rotation diagrams** | Position Coaching screen | Top-down half-court | [Part 3](#part-3--position-rotation-diagrams) |

---

## How to use this book

1. **Always prepend the [Global Style Block](#global-style-block).** It is what
   makes all 30+ images look like one set instead of a random pile. Paste it in
   front of any individual prompt below.
2. **Append the [Negative Prompt + Tech Specs](#negative-prompt--technical-specs)**
   (or set them as your tool's negative-prompt / aspect-ratio fields).
3. **Lock the look across the whole set.** Use the *same seed* and the *same
   model/version* for every image so colors, line weight, and the court style
   stay identical. In Midjourney add `--seed 12345 --style raw`; in DALL·E keep
   one chat thread and say "same style as before."
4. **Generate the template, then the variants.** e.g. generate one "across-the-net
   game", then re-run swapping "3 vs 3" for "2 vs 2".
5. **Keep text minimal.** Image models misspell. Prefer **numbers and 1–2 letter
   labels baked in**, and add longer captions later in your own layout. Where a
   word matters, the prompt asks for a clean sans-serif and you should proofread
   the result.

## Global Style Block

> **Paste this at the start of every prompt.**

```
A clean, modern instructional sports diagram in a friendly flat-vector style,
designed for a youth volleyball coaching app. Minimal, uncluttered, and easy to
read on a phone. Soft rounded shapes, generous negative space, crisp even line
weights. Warm, encouraging palette: a coral/orange brand accent (#FF6B4A) for
"your team", a calm teal-blue (#2BB3C0) for the other team or secondary
elements, warm neutral grey for the court and lines, and an off-white background.
Players shown as simple flat colored discs or minimal rounded figures (no faces,
no jerseys, no logos). Motion shown with smooth tapered arrows. A real
volleyball drawn as a simple white sphere with two curved seam lines. Subtle soft
shadows, gentle depth, high contrast for accessibility. Cohesive icon-set look,
vector illustration, infographic clarity. No photorealism.
```

### Negative Prompt + Technical Specs

```
Negative prompt: photo, photorealistic, 3D render, busy background, clutter,
gradients overload, neon, harsh shadows, drop-shadow text, watermark, signature,
brand logos, real faces, distorted anatomy, extra limbs, misspelled text,
gibberish text, cramped layout, low contrast, dark muddy colors.
```

- **Aspect ratio:** `4:3` for top-down court cards, `5:3` (≈ `300×180`) for the
  side-view motion scenes, `1:1` for the rotation wheel and breath. Midjourney:
  `--ar 4:3`. DALL·E: ask for "landscape".
- **Background:** flat off-white or transparent. Ask for "transparent PNG" where
  your tool supports it so the card re-themes cleanly in light/dark mode.
- **Resolution:** request the largest the tool offers; these are simple enough to
  upscale crisply.

### A reusable "court" phrase

Several prompts reuse a top-down court. Wherever a prompt says **`[COURT]`**,
substitute:

```
Top-down (bird's-eye) view of a single volleyball court drawn as a soft rounded
rectangle in warm grey on off-white, the net as a clean dashed line straight
across the middle with a small post dot at each end, and a thin attack (3-meter)
line on each side. Near side at the bottom, far side at the top.
```

# Part 1 — Drill court diagrams (top-down)

These replace the `RR.diagram` SVGs generated by the `RR.dk` builders. There are
**12 base templates** plus a couple of hand-built scenes. Generate each once,
then vary the count/labels noted under "Variations."

---

### 1.1 Serving at targets — `serveTargets`

> Servers behind the end line fire across the net into highlighted landing zones.

```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK]
[COURT]
Along the bottom end line, 3 coral player discs each labeled "S", every player
standing at their own spot (not in a line). One smooth coral arrow arcs from a
server up and over the net into a highlighted landing zone on the far court. On
the far side, 2 soft-highlighted rectangular target zones glowing gently (light
coral fill, rounded corners), each with a big friendly number ("1" and "5").
A small legend: coral disc = "Servers", highlighted box = "Aim here". Calm,
spacious, confident. --ar 4:3
```

**Variations** (re-run changing only these):
- **Number of servers:** 1 to 5 coral "S" discs spread along the end line.
- **Target zones:** 1 big forgiving zone ("land it in"); or 3 zones labeled
  "1/2/3" for around-the-world; or two "10"-point boxes deep in the corners.
- **Knockout / dead-fish / relay** feel: keep 5 servers, drop extra zones.

---

### 1.2 Two teams across the net — `acrossNet`

> A live small-sided game: fixed teams on each side, optional players waiting.

```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK]
[COURT]
A small-sided game in progress. On the near (bottom) half, 3 coral player discs
arranged in a triangle (two back, one front). On the far (top) half, 3 teal
player discs mirrored. Players sit at realistic court positions, evenly spaced.
A couple of small neutral grey discs wait just off the bottom sideline ("next
up"). Legend: coral = "Your side", teal = "Other side", grey = "Waiting". Balanced,
game-ready, easy to count at a glance. --ar 4:3
```

**Variations:**
- **Team size:** 1 vs 1, 2 vs 2, 3 vs 3, or 4 vs 4 (mirror both sides).
- **Court width:** full width, or a narrow half-width court ("line battle"), or a
  short court between attack line and net (mini / two-touch / volley-tennis).
- **Waiting players:** 0, 2, or 3 grey discs off the sideline; drop them for a
  cooperative "beat the number" version.

---

### 1.3 King / Queen of the Court — `rotateIn`

> Winners cross over and stay; losers go to the back of the waiting line.

```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK]
[COURT]
A "King/Queen of the Court" rotation. Near (bottom) half: 2 coral discs labeled
as the reigning team. Far (top) half: 2 teal "challenger" discs. The bottom
scoring half is gently highlighted in soft green with a small label "Score only
here". Two curved motion arrows tell the story: one arcs from the far side over
to the near side labeled "win → cross over", one arcs from the near side down to
a queue labeled "lose → back of line". Behind the far baseline, 3 small grey
discs wait in line. Legend: coral = "Kings/Queens", teal = "Challengers", grey =
"Waiting teams". --ar 4:3
```

**Variations:** team size 2 or 3 per side; 2–4 waiting teams.

---

### 1.4 Passing / control circle — `circlePass`

> One ball kept alive around a ring; nobody touches it twice in a row.

```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK]
6 coral player discs evenly spaced in a wide ring on an off-white background (no
court needed). Smooth curved ball-path arrows connect each player to the next one
clockwise around the circle, forming a continuous loop. A single white volleyball
with seam lines travels along one of the arrows. Open, cooperative, rhythmic feel.
Optional: one teal disc in the center for the "monarch/feeder" variant.
--ar 1:1
```

**Variations:** 6 or 7 discs (bigger "amoeba" blob); add/remove the center disc.

---

### 1.5 Partner lines facing each other — `pairsRows`

> Pairs face off and send one ball back and forth (pepper, set-and-catch).

```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK]
3 pairs of players facing each other in neat columns on off-white. In each pair,
one coral disc at the bottom (labeled "P") faces one teal disc at the top
(labeled "F"), with a gentle two-way curved ball arrow between them. Tidy,
repeatable, lots-of-reps feel. Optional net variant: add a clean dashed net line
across the middle with end posts and thin attack lines, pairs facing across it.
--ar 4:3
```

**Variations:** 2 or 3 pairs; with-net (over-the-net pepper, mini-court rally) or
no-net (set-and-catch); clear the "P"/"F" labels for a cleaner look.

---

### 1.6 Station rotation grid — `stations`

> A grid of skill stations with a rotation arrow looping through them in order.

```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK]
A clean 2x2 grid of four rounded rectangle "station" cards on off-white, each a
soft neutral panel with a big friendly number 1, 2, 3, 4. A smooth coral arrow
curves from station 1 to 2 to 3 to 4 and loops back to 1, showing the rotation
direction. Small legend: arrow = "Rotate this way". Organized, calm, classroom-
clear. --ar 4:3
```

**Variations:** 4 or 6 stations (2x3 or 3x2 grid); swap numbers for short labels
("Serve", "Pass", "Set", "Hit") — proofread the spelling.

---

### 1.7 Coach feeding back-court defenders — `coachFeed`

> A coach near the net tosses/hits balls down to a line of diggers.

```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK]
Top-down half-court (net as a dashed line near the top with end posts). At the
top center, one distinct coach disc labeled "C" with a small note "coach feeds".
Across the back of the court, 3 coral defender discs labeled "D" evenly spaced.
A couple of smooth coral ball arrows fan out from the coach down to the
defenders. Legend: coach disc = "Coach", coral = "Defenders". Focused defensive-
reps feel. --ar 4:3
```

**Variations:** 2–4 defenders; change the coach note ("coach tips", "coach hits").

---

### 1.8 Working a wall — `wall`

> One or more players rebound a ball off a wall and reset.

```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK]
A thick horizontal "WALL" bar (neutral grey, labeled "WALL") across the top of an
off-white frame. Below it, 3 coral player discs in a row facing the wall. From
the center player, one coral ball arrow goes up to the wall labeled "off the
wall", and a second dashed movement arrow returns down labeled with the reset
step. Simple, repetitive, solo-reps feel. --ar 4:3
```

**Variations:** 1–4 players along the wall.

---

### 1.9 Feed line ("lines" drill) — `feedLine`

> Feeder sends a ball to the front player, who plays it to a target, then jogs to
> the back of the line.

```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK]
Top-down court. Near the top center, a coach/feeder disc labeled "C" ("feeder").
In the middle, one active coral disc labeled "1" ("your turn"). To the right, a
target disc labeled "T" ("target"). In the bottom-left corner, a short stack of
3 grey discs ("line waits"). Three arrows tell the sequence: a coral ball arrow
from feeder to the active player ("feed"), a coral ball arrow from the active
player to the target ("play it"), and a dashed grey movement arrow curving from
the active player to the back of the line ("to back of line"). Legend: coach =
"Feeder", coral = "Worker + target", grey = "Line waits". --ar 4:3
```

**Variations:** queue of 2–4; relabel the action ("dig", "set", "hit"); some
versions drop the target disc.

---

### 1.10 Down-and-back lanes (warm-up) — `lanes`

> Players move baseline to baseline in parallel lanes.

```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK]
Top-down court with a thin center line across the middle. Along the bottom
baseline, 4 coral player discs each in their own vertical lane. From each player
a straight movement arrow runs up the court to the far baseline (parallel lanes).
For the "down and back" version, add a second set of return arrows beside them.
Legend: arrow = "Move this way" (or "Down and back"). Crisp, orderly, warm-up
energy. --ar 4:3
```

**Variations:** 3–5 lanes; one-way or down-and-back.

---

### 1.11 Hitter's approach footwork — `approachPath`

> One attacker's stepping path into the net, the set arriving, and the swing.

```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK]
Top-down court with the net as a dashed line across the top. One coral hitter disc
labeled "H" near the back ("start"). A teal setter disc labeled "St" near the net
center ("setter"). A two-segment dashed movement arrow shows the approach
footwork curving from the hitter up toward a takeoff point at the net, labeled
"approach". A coral ball arrow from the setter meets the takeoff point ("set"),
and a bold accent arrow snaps from the takeoff over the net into the far court
("swing"). Dynamic but clean. --ar 4:3
```

**Variations:** approach from **left**, **middle**, or **right**; with or without
the setter; with or without the finishing "swing" arrow.

---

### 1.12 Six base court positions (defense / serve-receive) — `basePositions`

> The six standard floor spots, often with a coach attacking from across the net.

```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK]
Top-down half-court, net as a dashed line near the top with end posts and an
attack line. Six coral player discs in the standard base defensive shape: two at
the net (front-left and front-right), one in the middle, and three across the
back (left, center, right). At the top center, a coach disc labeled "C" with a
note "coach attacks", and a faint ball arrow coming toward the floor. Legend:
coral = "Base spots", coach = "Attack". Balanced, textbook-clear. --ar 4:3
```

**Variations:** label the six discs with role abbreviations; drop the coach for a
pure "where everyone stands" version.

### 1.13 Hand-built scene — Free-ball mini-game (example of a play sequence)

> A few drills use a custom scene instead of a template. This is the model for
> any "run a full pass→set→hit play" picture.

```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK]
[COURT]
A 3-vs-3 free-ball rally. Far (top) side: 3 teal defender discs spread to receive.
Near (bottom) side: 3 coral discs labeled "P" (passer), "S" (setter), "H"
(hitter). In the top-right corner, a coach disc labeled "C" ("tosses"). A
sequence of labeled arrows tells the whole play in order: an accent arrow arcs
from the coach to the passer ("free ball"); a ball arrow from passer to setter
("pass"); a ball arrow from setter to hitter ("set"); a bold accent arrow from
the hitter over the net into the far court ("attack"). Legend: coral = "Your side
(P→S→H)", teal = "Other side", coach = "Coach (free ball)". Reads like a clear
play sequence. --ar 4:3
```

---

### Note on multi-step drills (`dk.seq`)

Many skill drills are shown as a **2–3 step sequence** (Step 1, Step 2, …), each
step being one of the templates above. To illustrate these, generate each step as
its own image using the matching template prompt, and **lay them side by side with
"Step 1 / Step 2 / Step 3" headers** in your own layout. Keep the same seed across
the steps so they look like one storyboard.

# Part 2 — Tips motion diagrams (side-view)

These replace the animated SVGs on the **Tips** screen. They are **side-on action
scenes** on a 5:3 canvas with a floor line near the bottom. The original versions
animate a ball along the trajectory — for a still image, draw the **ball's path as
a tapered dotted trail with the ball near the end**, so the motion still reads.
Use a single stylized athlete (no face), the brand palette, and a clean floor.

> **Shared add-on for every Part 2 prompt** (append after the Global Style Block):
> `Side-on (profile) view. A horizontal floor line near the bottom. One simple,
> faceless stylized athlete (head + rounded body, brand coral). The ball's flight
> shown as a smooth tapered dotted trail with a white seamed volleyball near the
> end of the path. 5:3 landscape, lots of clean negative space. --ar 5:3`

### The six core skills

**2.1 Serving**
```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK] [PART-2 ADD-ON]
The athlete stands on the left in a calm overhand serving posture, tossing arm up.
A dotted trail rises from the hand, peaks high, and arcs over a net (vertical
dashed line, right of center) down into a soft target ellipse on the far floor.
Conveys: a calm toss, full arm swing, ball clears the net into open court.
```

**2.2 Passing (forearm)**
```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK] [PART-2 ADD-ON]
The athlete is low with a flat forearm "platform" angled toward a target. A dotted
trail comes in from the upper right (over a net on the left), drops to the
platform, and pops up gently toward a small target ellipse near the net. Conveys:
read the serve, flat angled platform, pop it up to the setter.
```

**2.3 Setting**
```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK] [PART-2 ADD-ON]
The athlete stands center with both hands up in a relaxed triangle above the
forehead. A dotted trail comes in low from the left, rises into the hands, then
pushes up and out to the right toward a target ellipse. Conveys: take the ball
high in a triangle, push through the legs out to the hitter.
```

**2.4 Hitting (spike)**
```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK] [PART-2 ADD-ON]
Three small footprints on the floor (left–right–left approach) lead up to the
athlete jumping near a net (vertical dashed line, center). A dotted trail snaps
sharply downward from the high contact point over the net into a target ellipse on
the far floor. Conveys: timed approach, high contact, snap it down.
```

**2.5 Blocking**
```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK] [PART-2 ADD-ON]
The athlete is at the net (vertical dashed line, center) with both hands pressed
high over the top of the net. A dotted trail comes in from the left toward the
hands and deflects straight back down on the same side. Conveys: press the hands
over, watch the hitter, turn the attack straight down.
```

**2.6 Digging**
```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK] [PART-2 ADD-ON]
The athlete is very low on the right with a flat platform near the floor. A dotted
trail drives in hard and flat from the upper left, meets the platform, and lifts
back up high toward a target ellipse near center. Conveys: stay low, platform to
the ball, lift the hard-driven attack back up.
```

> Generate all six with the **same seed and athlete style** so they read as a
> matched skill set.

# Part 3 — Position rotation diagrams

These replace `RR.positions.rotationSpec` — a top-down half-court showing the six
rotation zones with **one position's usual starting zone highlighted**. Generate
the **base** once, then re-run highlighting the right zone(s) for each role.

### 3.1 Base rotation-zone diagram

```
[GLOBAL STYLE BLOCK]
Top-down half-court as a soft rounded rectangle, a dashed net line across the top
with two end posts, and a thin attack (3-meter) line. Six evenly spaced position
circles in the standard layout: front row near the net = zones 4, 3, 2 (left to
right); back row = zones 5, 6, 1 (left to right). Each circle shows its zone
number in a clean sans-serif. All circles neutral grey in the base version. A
small legend noting players rotate clockwise 1 → 6 → 5 → 4 → 3 → 2. Calm,
textbook-clear, phone-readable. --ar 1:1
```

### 3.2 Per-position variants

Re-run 3.1, but render the highlighted "usual start" zone(s) as **coral** (with a
small role abbreviation beside the number) and the rest neutral grey:

| Position | Abbr | Highlight zone(s) | Add to prompt |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Setter** | S | zone 1 | "Highlight zone 1 in coral, labeled S — back-right, the setter's base." |
| **Outside hitter** | OH | zone 4 | "Highlight zone 4 in coral, labeled OH — front-left pin." |
| **Opposite** | OPP | zone 2 | "Highlight zone 2 in coral, labeled OPP — front-right, opposite the setter." |
| **Middle blocker** | MB | zone 3 | "Highlight zone 3 in coral, labeled MB — front-middle at the net." |
| **Libero** | L | zones 5 & 6 | "Highlight zones 5 and 6 in coral, labeled L — back-row defense." |
| **Defensive specialist** | DS | zones 5 & 6 | "Highlight zones 5 and 6 in coral, labeled DS — back-row defense." |

> Verify the exact start zones against `js/positions-data.js` (`startZones`) if you
> want them to match the app precisely; the table above reflects the current data.

## Keeping the whole set consistent (quick checklist)

- [ ] Same model + **same seed** for every image.
- [ ] Global Style Block prepended every time.
- [ ] Negative prompt applied every time.
- [ ] Coral = your team, Teal = other team, Grey = waiting/neutral — never mixed up.
- [ ] Net always a dashed line with end-post dots; near side at the bottom.
- [ ] Ball always the same simple white two-seam sphere.
- [ ] Numbers/short labels baked in; long captions added later in your layout.
- [ ] Export transparent PNG (or off-white) so cards work in light **and** dark mode.
- [ ] Proofread any in-image text before shipping.
